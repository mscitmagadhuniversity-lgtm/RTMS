About the University

Magadh University, Bodh Gaya, was founded on March 1, 1962, through the vision and dedication of scholars and educationists from the Magadh region. Its first Vice-Chancellor was Prof. (Dr.) K. K. Dutta, an eminent historian and academic of international standing. The establishment of the University’s campus became possible due to the generous donation of land by Shri Satanand Giri, the revered Mahant of Bodh Gaya Math, at the request of the then Education Minister. On August 20, 1964, the foundation stone of the present campus was laid by Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, the then President of India.

Today, Magadh University is recognized as one of Bihar’s leading institutions of higher education. It offers postgraduate programs in 22 disciplines across the faculties of Science, Social Science, Humanities, and Commerce. Alongside traditional courses, the University provides a diverse range of vocational and professional programs in fields such as Education, Physiotherapy, Information Technology, Management, Environmental Science, Library Science, and Biotechnology. With its enduring commitment to academic excellence, research, and holistic growth, the University continues to be a hub of knowledge and innovation, nurturing future scholars and professionals. At present, the Vice-Chancellor, Prof. Shashi Pratap Shahi, is actively working to restore and enhance the University’s proud legacy by reinforcing its focus on quality education and encouraging faculty members to engage in conferences, seminars, and workshops at both national and international platforms.

About the Department

The Postgraduate Department of Mathematics at Magadh University was established in 1962. In its formative years, classes were conducted in a rented building, as the University had not yet developed its own infrastructure. The Department’s journey began with the appointment of Dr. Shyam Sundar Prasad, who joined after being transferred from Nalanda College, Bihar Sharif. He was soon joined by eminent academicians including Dr. R. Shukla (Professor & Head), Dr. Shaligram Singh (Reader), Dr. Ziaul Hasan (Lecturer), and Dr. P. Tiwari (Lecturer).

With the relocation of the University to its permanent campus at Bodh Gaya on December 15, 1969, the Department of Mathematics also moved into a newly constructed facility. The spacious and well-equipped environment provided a strong foundation for advancing both teaching and research activities.

Over the years, the Department has gained recognition for its noteworthy contributions to diverse fields such as Set Theory, Topology, Algebra, Fuzzy Mathematics, Relativity Theory, Fixed Point Theory, Topological Dynamics, Bio-Mathematics, and Differential Equations. Alongside postgraduate programs, the Department offers research opportunities leading to Ph.D. and D.Sc. degrees, nurturing scholars who have made impactful contributions to the growth of mathematical sciences.

At present, the Department has nine dedicated faculty members whose research and teaching expertise cover a wide range of subjects in both Pure and Applied Mathematics, ensuring a vibrant academic environment for learners and researchers alike.

About the City

Gaya (Gayaji) is an ancient cultural centre drawing pilgrims and scholars worldwide. Nearby Bodh Gaya is the place of Lord Buddha's enlightenment, making the region globally significant for pilgrims and researchers alike.

About the Conference

The conference aims to serve as a dynamic platform for academicians, researchers and industry experts to share, exchange, and disseminate their knowledge on recent developments and emerging trends in Pure Mathematics, Applied Mathematics, Computer Science, Physics, and Chemistry. A special focus is placed on motivating and engaging young researchers and students.
